Paradigms of
Machine Learning

— the three frameworks that organize everything else in this course —

01

Supervised Learning

Data arrives with labels from a teacher — the model learns to map inputs to known answers.

Classification

Predicts a label from a finite set.

Real Life

An email inbox sorting spam from non-spam.

Analogy

A teacher marking papers pass/fail using a fixed answer key.

Regression

Predicts a continuous numerical value.

Real Life

Forecasting tomorrow's rainfall in millimeters.

Analogy

A doctor estimating your age from an X-ray — not a category, a number on a sliding scale.

Ranking

Orders a set of items by relevance.

Real Life

A search engine ordering web pages by relevance to your query.

Analogy

A talent-show judge ranking contestants from best to worst, not just picking one winner.

Structure Learning

Predicts complex outputs like trees or structures.

Real Life

Predicting a sentence's grammatical parse tree, or a molecule's 3D shape.

Analogy

An architect predicting a full blueprint from a client's requirements — not one number, a whole structure.

02

Unsupervised Learning

No labels, no teacher — the model has to find the hidden structure on its own.

Clustering

Groups objects automatically based on similarity.

Real Life

Your phone grouping photos of the same person, without ever being told their name.

Analogy

Sorting a mixed basket of fruit by type — no labels needed, similarity does the sorting.

Representation Learning

Learns the most efficient way to represent data.

Real Life

Image compression that keeps a photo recognizable at a fraction of the file size.

Analogy

An artist's quick sketch that captures the essence of a subject in five lines instead of a full portrait.

03

Sequential Learning

Learning unfolds over time, through a chain of decisions and feedback.

Not covered in this course — included here for context only.

Online Learning

Learns from feedback across a stream of predictions.

Real Life

A stock-prediction model updating itself after every trading day.

Analogy

A trader adjusting strategy after every single trade, based on how it played out.

Multi-Armed Bandits

Deals with partial feedback — you only learn about the action you took.

Real Life

An app A/B-testing which button color gets more clicks.

Analogy

Trying restaurants in a new city — you only find out how good tonight's pick was, never the ones you skipped.

Reinforcement Learning

An agent learns a policy through interaction with an environment.

Real Life

A robot learning to navigate a room, avoiding obstacles through trial and error.

Analogy

A dog learning tricks through treats and corrections — no instructions, just consequences.

Prerequisites

📐

Linear Algebra

Understanding the geometry of data — vectors, matrices, spaces.

Like reading architectural blueprints: knowing how dimensions and structure relate before you build anything.

🎲

Probability

The mathematical foundation for handling noise and uncertainty.

Like a weather forecaster giving a percentage chance of rain instead of a flat yes or no.

📈

Optimization

Making data-driven decisions by minimizing or maximizing a metric.

Like a hiker choosing the most efficient trail to the summit, out of dozens of possible paths.
