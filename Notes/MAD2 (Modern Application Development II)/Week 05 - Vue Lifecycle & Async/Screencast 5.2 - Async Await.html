Sync by Default, Async by Choice

JS Is Fundamentally Synchronous

Real Life

Reading a recipe top to bottom, one step fully finished before starting the next.

Analogy

Async features are the "place an order and keep cooking" escape hatch for the few steps — like waiting for dough to rise — that would otherwise stall the whole recipe.

Promises: A Tracking Number, Not the Package

Ordering online hands you a tracking number immediately — not the package. The Promise is that tracking number: a placeholder for a value that isn't here yet.

pending — "in transit" → fulfilled — "delivered"
pending — "in transit" → rejected — "lost in transit"

.then()

Runs once the Promise resolves successfully.

Real Life

Opening the box the moment it's delivered and actually using what's inside.

.catch()

Runs if the Promise rejects — something went wrong.

Real Life

Filing a damage claim if the package arrives broken, or never arrives at all.

await: A Scoped Pause

await Pauses Just This Function

Real Life

Standing at the pickup counter waiting for your specific package to be called — you're paused, but the rest of the store keeps serving other customers just fine.

Analogy

await only blocks the async function it's written in — it doesn't freeze the whole call stack the way a long synchronous loop does.

Only Inside async

Real Life

You can only stand at "the pickup counter" if you're actually the customer with an order in the system — bystanders can't just wait there for nothing.

Analogy

await needs an async function's context to make sense — with rare top-level exceptions in some environments.

Two Styles of Error Handling

Promise-Based: .catch()

A damage-claim form attached directly to that one specific order — chained right onto the delivery you're tracking.

Async/Await: try...catch

A general safety net wrapped around the whole counter visit — if anything goes wrong while you're waiting, the catch block steps in immediately, same as handling errors in ordinary synchronous code.

A Promise is a receipt for something not here yet; await is choosing to wait for that one receipt without shutting down everything else going on.
