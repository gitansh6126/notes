Estimation in ML: MLE, Bayesian estimation, Gaussian Mixture Models, and the EM algorithm.

Estimation · GMM · EM Algorithm

1. What is "estimation"?

Infer unknown distribution parameters from observed data, assuming points are IID (Independent and Identically Distributed). Goal: characterize the data-generating distribution well enough to explain it — or generate new samples from it.

2. MLE

frequentist

Pick parameters that make the observed data most probable under the model. No prior beliefs involved — purely data-driven.

θ̂ = argmax_θ P(data | θ)

Bernoulli(p): MLE p̂ = sample mean

Gaussian(μ,σ²): MLE μ̂, σ̂² = sample mean, sample variance

3. Bayesian estimation

prior-aware

Combines prior belief with observed evidence to get a full posterior distribution over parameters — not just a point estimate.

Posterior ∝ Prior × Likelihood

Beta prior + Bernoulli likelihood → Beta posterior (conjugacy keeps the math closed-form).

4. Gaussian Mixture Models (GMM)

Model complex, multi-modal data as a weighted sum of K Gaussians. Each component = one soft cluster.

P(x) = Σₖ πₖ · N(x | μₖ, σₖ²) where Σₖ πₖ = 1

vs K-means

K-means: hard assignment — each point belongs to exactly one cluster.

GMM: soft assignment — each point gets a probability of belonging to each cluster (responsibility).

probabilistic clusteringgeneralizes K-means

5. EM Algorithm

GMM's log-likelihood has a sum inside a log — not directly optimizable via calculus. EM sidesteps this by alternating two steps.

log P(X|θ) = Σᵢ log( Σₖ πₖ N(xᵢ|μₖ,σₖ²) ) ← hard to maximize directly
E

E-step (Expectation)

Fix current θ = (μ,σ²,π). Compute responsibility λ_ik = P(point i is from cluster k | current params), using Jensen's inequality to form a tractable lower bound on the log-likelihood.

M

M-step (Maximization)

Fix the responsibilities λ_ik from E-step. Re-estimate μₖ, σₖ², πₖ by maximizing the (now tractable) expected log-likelihood.

E-step M-step repeat until Δθ < tolerance

EM is naturally soft clustering. For hard clustering, just take argmax_k λ_ik per point — same trick as turning GMM into K-means-style output.
